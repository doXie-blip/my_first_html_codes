HSL(Hue, Saturation,Lightness) is an intuitive color model used in HTML and CSS to define colors based on how humans perceive them.
an example can be seen at the beginning of the page where texts are in different colors such as "Hello world"

HUE refers to the color. the numbers range from 0-360.
Redis at 0
Greenis at 120 and
Blueis at 240

SATURATION is a percentage value controlling color intensity.
0% is a shade of gray, while
100% is the full, vivid color.

LIGHTNESS: A percentage value controlling brightness. 0% is completely black, 50% is normal brightness, and 100% is pure white.

HSLA (NOTE:alpha is at 0.4)

HSLA follows the same definition as the hsl from huedown to lightness.
The A in the HSLA standa for ALPHAwhich is a decimal fraction representing opacity/transparency,ranging from;
0.0=fully transparent (invinsible) to
1.o=fully opaque (solid)
